# Week 1 - Business Analysis Framework Primer

**Prepared by:** Ruiyuan Yin  
**Purpose:** Build a simple working vocabulary for analysing InGen's physical AI products in the following weeks.

## 1. SWOT for a physical AI company
I understand SWOT as a way to separate internal and external conditions. **Strengths** and **weaknesses** are mainly inside the company, while **opportunities** and **threats** come from the market. For InGen, one strength may be several robot platforms with a shared AI direction, so some research can be reused. A weakness is that many products are still in development or research stage, so customer proof and cost information are limited. Opportunities include labour shortage, remote inspection, eldercare support, and physical security. Threats include strong competitors, long sales cycles, safety incidents, and privacy concerns.
A SWOT should not become four boxes with general words. Each point should lead to an action, such as choosing a narrow pilot or collecting missing evidence. **Assumption:** InGen can transfer some technology and learning between platforms; if every product is separate, this strength becomes smaller.
## 2. TAM / SAM / SOM market sizing
TAM, SAM, and SOM reduce a large market statement into a more realistic target. **TAM** is all possible spending for the problem, not revenue one company will receive. **SAM** removes customers that cannot be served because of function, geography, regulation, or sales channel. **SOM** is the part that may be won in the first years, considering product readiness, competitors, and sales capacity.
For Sentinel, TAM may start from enterprise physical-security spending. SAM should focus on sites where autonomous sensing and alert support are suitable, such as industrial campuses. SOM should use reachable pilots and a realistic conversion rate, not simply a fixed percentage of TAM. **Assumption:** These customers have a real budget and willingness to change or support the present security process.
## 3. Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)
TCO means the robot price is only the first line of cost. A useful model should include hardware, shipping, payloads, site preparation, integration, software, energy, maintenance, battery replacement, training, human supervision, downtime, and end-of-life value. The time period must be the same, for example five years for Rover and the current manned patrol process.
Energy should be connected with useful output. Rover can be measured by energy and cost per completed patrol, while Sentinel can use cost per validated alert. This is more useful than electricity per hour. Unknown values should be marked as estimates and tested with low, base, and high cases. **Assumption:** Mission volume and service quality are comparable between the robot and human process.
## 4. Stakeholder map for B2B enterprise sales
In B2B sales, the customer is usually a group, not one person. A stakeholder map records each person's role, influence, concern, purchase objection, and needed evidence. For Sentinel, the Chief Security Officer may own the problem, the SOC manager uses the system, IT reviews integration, legal reviews data handling, finance checks return, and procurement controls the contract. Frontline guards also matter because they may support or resist the workflow change.
The map should show who can approve, who can block, and who operates the product after purchase. It is not enough to say everyone is “concerned about cost.” Each role needs a specific objection and response. **Assumption:** The economic buyer controls a budget and can coordinate other departments during a pilot.
## 5. Data analysis workflow
My basic workflow is: define the question, collect data, clean it, analyse patterns, explain the business meaning, then validate and communicate. The question should come first because collecting many numbers without a decision purpose wastes time. In Week 1, I separated product facts, competitor analogues, and analyst estimates, so an estimated cost tier does not look like an InGen quotation.
During cleaning, units, dates, product versions, missing values, and source quality should be checked. I should compare like with like and record why one proxy is selected. The output should include the finding, evidence, limitation, and next question, so another person can review it. Missing data should stay visible instead of becoming a confident number. **Assumption:** Public sources are current enough and the products are reasonably comparable.
